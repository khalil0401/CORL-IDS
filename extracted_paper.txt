2.4. Research Motivation
DespitethenotablesuccesofrecnetDeepReinforcement
Learning (DRL) and generative models in IoT Intrusion
detection , there is two critical gaps unaddressed . First ,
existing models heavily rely on external data augmentation
techniques such as GANs and SMOTE to mitigate extremeclass imbalance such as the U2R class in Jin et al. (2025)
, which artificially alters the original data distribution and
increasescomputationaloverhead.Second,whenmitigating
zero days attacks , current methos primarily rely on static
anomaly detection thresholds such as EVT in Yu et al.
(2024) that flag novel attacks as generic unknowns without
dynamicallylearningtheirsignatures,oftenresultinginhigh
miss rates.
Toaddressthesecriticallimitations,weproposesanovel
framework: Continual Open-Set Reinforcement Learning
withRarity-AwareDynamicRewardShaping.Handlingim-
balance via reward shaping, instead of preprocessing or
generating synthetic data, the reward function dynamically
scales the agent’s rewards to the class probability , this
natively forces the RL agent to prioritize minority classes
without altering the dataset.
Continual zero-day attack discovery, rather than stati-
cally flagging unknown traffic, the RL agent clustering and
learning new zero-day attacks as distinct classes without
forgetting previously known attacks.
By bridging dynamic reward shaping with continual
open-set learning, this framework aims to provide a highly
adaptive and suitable solution for dynamic IoT environ-
ments.
